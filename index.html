How to Check if Your Globe SIM is Registered: Quick Methods and Tips

As the Philippine government enforces SIM card registration as a part of its efforts to combat text scams and ensure better communication security, it is crucial for all TM SIM users to check whether their SIM cards are registered. If you fail to register your TM SIM, Globe SIM, or any other SIM card, you risk having it deactivated, rendering it unusable for texting, calling, and browsing. Fortunately, there are quick methods to check if your TM SIM is registered. In this detailed guide, we will walk you through these methods and offer helpful tips to make the registration process smooth.

Why Is SIM Card Registration Important?

The SIM card registration law, mandated by the Philippine government, aims to fight illegal activities, such as text scams, fraudulent messages, and other forms of cybercrime. By requiring SIM card owners to register their details, the government and telecom companies aim to create a safer digital environment.

For TM SIM and Globe users, this law mandates that all SIM cards, including prepaid, postpaid, and mobile broadband, must be registered. If your SIM is unregistered, it may be suspended or deactivated after the registration deadline.

Methods to Check If Your Globe or TM SIM is Registered

1. Check Using the Globe SIM Registration Website

Globe Telecom has provided an online portal where users can easily check the status of their SIM card registration. Here’s how to do it:

  • Open a web browser and go to the official Globe SIM Registration page: https://new.globe.com.ph/simreg.
  • Log in to your Globe account using your mobile number and One-Time PIN (OTP) if prompted.
  • Follow the instructions on the page, which will guide you to check your registration status.
  • If your SIM is already registered, you should see a confirmation message. Otherwise, you’ll be prompted to register your SIM.

2. Use the Globe USSD Code

If you prefer a quick method without needing internet access, you can use the Globe USSD code to check the status of your SIM registration.

  • Open your phone’s dialer.
  • Dial the USSD code *143#.
  • Select the option related to SIM registration or follow the instructions prompted on the screen.
  • You should see a message confirming whether your SIM is registered or not.

This method is especially useful if you don’t have access to a stable internet connection.

3. Check via SMS

Globe also allows users to check their SIM registration status via SMS. This is one of the most convenient ways to quickly verify the status without navigating through any websites or USSD menus.

  • Open your messaging app and send an SMS to "8080" with the word "REGISTER" in the message body.
  • Wait for a reply. If your SIM is already registered, you will receive a confirmation message. If it’s not registered, the response will guide you on how to complete the registration process.

This SMS method is particularly handy for those who may not be comfortable with internet browsing.

4. Contact Globe Customer Support

If you’ve tried the above methods and still aren’t sure whether your TM SIM is registered, you can contact Globe customer support for assistance.

  • Call Globe’s customer support hotline at 211 (for Globe users).
  • Explain that you would like to check the registration status of your SIM.
  • Be ready to provide the required details, such as your Globe mobile number, so the representative can assist you in checking your SIM registration status.

Globe customer support will give you up-to-date information regarding your SIM registration status and guide you if any additional steps are needed.

5. Use the GlobeOne App

The GlobeOne app offers a variety of features, including account management, bill payments, and checking the status of your SIM registration.

  • Download the GlobeOne app from the Google Play Store or Apple App Store, if you haven’t already.
  • Open the app and log in using your Globe account.
  • Go to the "SIM Registration" section to view your SIM registration status.
  • If your SIM is registered, the app will display confirmation. If it is not, you can begin the registration process through the app.

This method is especially useful for users who manage all their Globe services via the app and prefer a more seamless experience.

Tips for Ensuring Your SIM is Registered

1. Complete the Registration Process Immediately

If you find out that your SIM is not registered, it’s essential to complete the registration process immediately to avoid deactivation. You can do this via the Globe SIM Registration portal, SMS, or customer support channels.

2. Prepare Your Valid ID

In line with the SIM registration requirements, Globe will ask for a valid ID during registration. Ensure you have a government-issued ID (e.g., passport, driver’s license, or voter’s ID) ready to make the process quicker.

3. Keep Your Contact Information Updated

During the registration process, make sure the information you provide is accurate. If your contact details change in the future (e.g., new address or contact number), it’s advisable to update your SIM registration details. This ensures you don’t miss important notifications from Globe or government agencies.

4. Monitor Official Globe Announcements

Keep an eye on official Globe announcements regarding SIM registration deadlines. If you don’t register by the deadline, your SIM may be deactivated, and you will lose access to mobile services.

5. Watch Out for Fake Registration Links

As the registration deadline approaches, there may be fraudulent websites or text messages asking you to click on links to register your SIM. Always make sure you’re on the official Globe website or using the official Globe registration channels.

Conclusion

Checking if your TM SIM is registered is simple and quick, thanks to the variety of methods available. Whether you prefer using a web portal, dialing a USSD code, sending an SMS, or using the GlobeOne app, you can easily confirm your SIM card’s registration status. Don’t risk losing your mobile services — take action and verify your registration today. By following the steps and tips outlined in this guide, you’ll ensure that your TM SIM remains active and compliant with the government’s SIM registration law.
